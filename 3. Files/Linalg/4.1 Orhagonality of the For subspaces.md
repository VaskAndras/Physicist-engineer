## 1. Projection onto a Line
- **Goal:** Find the point $p$ on the line through $a$ that is *closest* to $b$.
- **Key condition:** The error $e = b - p$ is *orthogonal* to $a$:
  $$
  a^T (b - p) = 0
  $$
- **Since** $p = x a$, substitute:
  $$
  a^T (b - x a) = 0 \Rightarrow x = \frac{a^T b}{a^T a}
  $$
- **Projection vector:**
  $$
  p = \frac{a^T b}{a^T a} \, a
  $$
- **Projection matrix:**
  $$
  P = \frac{a a^T}{a^T a}, \quad \text{so } p = P b
  $$
- **Properties:**
  - $P$ is symmetric: $P^T = P$
  - $P$ is idempotent: $P^2 = P$
  - Projects onto $\text{span}\{a\}$  
  - $(I - P)$ projects onto the subspace orthogonal to $a$

## 2. Projection onto a Subspace
- **Given:** $A = [a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n]$ with linearly independent columns.
- **We seek:** $p = A x$ in $\text{C}(A)$ (the column space of $A$) closest to $b$.
- **Orthogonality condition:**  
  The error $e = b - A x$ is orthogonal to all columns of $A$:
  $$
  A^T (b - A x) = 0
  $$
- **Normal equations:**
  $$
  A^T A x = A^T b
  $$
- **Solve for** $x$:
  $$
  x = (A^T A)^{-1} A^T b
  $$
- **Projection vector:**
  $$
  p = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T b
  $$
- **Projection matrix:**
  $$
  P = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T
  $$

---

## 3. Properties of the Projection Matrix
- **Symmetry:** $P^T = P$
- **Idempotence:** $P^2 = P$
- **Range:** $\text{C}(P) = \text{C}(A)$
- **Nullspace:** $\text{N}(P) = \text{N}(A^T)$
- **Decomposition:** For any $b$,
  $$
  b = p + e = P b + (I - P) b
  $$
  where $p \in \text{C}(A)$ and $e \in \text{N}(A^T)$, and they are orthogonal:
  $$
  p^T e = 0
  $$

---

## 4. Invertibility of $A^T A$
- $A^T A$ is **square** ($n \times n$), **symmetric**, and:
  $$
  \text{Invertible} \iff \text{columns of } A \text{ are linearly independent.}
  $$
- **Proof idea:**
  - If $A x = 0$, then $A^T A x = 0$.
  - Conversely, if $A^T A x = 0$, then
    $$
    x^T (A^T A) x = \|A x\|^2 = 0 \Rightarrow A x = 0.
    $$
  - Hence, $\text{N}(A^T A) = \text{N}(A)$.

---

## 5. Special Cases
- **Projection onto a line** (one column): $A = a$
  $$
  P = \frac{a a^T}{a^T a}
  $$
- **Projection onto a plane** (two columns in $\mathbb{R}^3$):
  $$
  P = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T, \quad \text{rank}(P) = 2
  $$
- **Complementary projection:**
  $$
  I - P \text{ projects onto } \text{N}(A^T)
  $$

---

## 6. Geometric Interpretation
- Projection *splits* any vector $b$ into orthogonal components:
  $$
  b = p + e
  $$
  where  
  $p$ = part **in** the subspace $\text{C}(A)$  
  $e$ = part **perpendicular** to it ($e \in \text{N}(A^T)$)
- This forms a **right triangle** in $\mathbb{R}^m$:
  $$
  \|b\|^2 = \|p\|^2 + \|e\|^2
  $$

---

## 7. Summary of Key Formulas
1. **Normal equation:** $A^T A x = A^T b$  
2. **Best coefficients:** $x = (A^T A)^{-1} A^T b$  
3. **Projection vector:** $p = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T b$  
4. **Projection matrix:** $P = A (A^T A)^{-1} A^T$  
5. **Error vector:** $e = b - p = (I - P) b$  
6. **Orthogonality:** $A^T e = 0$
