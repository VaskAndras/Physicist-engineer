# 3.5 Dimensions of the Four Subspaces

## Key Facts
1. Both the **column space** $C(A)$ and **row space** $C(A^T)$ have **dimension $r$**, the **rank** of $A$.
2. The **nullspace** $N(A)$ has **dimension $n - r$**.  
   The **left nullspace** $N(A^T)$ has **dimension $m - r$**.
3. **Elimination (row reduction)** gives bases for the **row space** and **nullspace** of $A$.
   - These are the same as for the reduced row echelon form $R$.
4. **Elimination changes** the column space and left nullspace,  
   but **their dimensions stay the same**.
5. **Rank-one matrices** $A = uv^T$:
   - $C(A)$ has basis $u$
   - $C(A^T)$ has basis $v$

---

## The Four Fundamental Subspaces
Each matrix $A$ defines four important subspaces:

| Subspace | Symbol | Space it belongs to | Dimension |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|------------|
| Row space | $C(A^T)$ | $\mathbb{R}^n$ | $r$ |
| Column space | $C(A)$ | $\mathbb{R}^m$ | $r$ |
| Nullspace | $N(A)$ | $\mathbb{R}^n$ | $n - r$ |
| Left nullspace | $N(A^T)$ | $\mathbb{R}^m$ | $m - r$ |

- **Row space**: combinations of rows of $A$, i.e., column space of $A^T$.
- **Left nullspace**: solutions to $A^T y = 0$, i.e., vectors orthogonal to the rows of $A$.

---

## Fundamental Theorem of Linear Algebra (Part 1)

1. **Dimensions**:
   - $\dim(C(A)) = \dim(C(A^T)) = r$
   - $\dim(N(A)) = n - r$
   - $\dim(N(A^T)) = m - r$
2. **Counting rules**:
   - In $\mathbb{R}^n$: $r + (n - r) = n$
   - In $\mathbb{R}^m$: $r + (m - r) = m$
3. **Interpretation**:  
   - The **rank** tells how many independent directions remain after elimination.  
   - The **nullity** ($n - r$) tells how many degrees of freedom remain in $x$.

---

## The Four Subspaces for the Echelon Form $R$
Let $R$ be the row echelon form of $A$.  
Example: $m=3, n=5, r=2$

1. **Row space**
   - Dimension: $r = 2$
   - Basis: nonzero rows (pivot rows)
   - The row space is unchanged by elimination.

2. **Column space**
   - Dimension: $r = 2$
   - Basis: pivot columns
   - Free columns are combinations of pivot columns.

3. **Nullspace**
   - Dimension: $n - r = 3$
   - Free variables → special solutions
   - Basis: one special solution per free variable

4. **Left nullspace**
   - Dimension: $m - r = 1$
   - Solve $R^T y = 0$
   - Corresponds to zero rows in $R$
   - In general: if $A$ is $m \times n$ of rank $r$,  
     then $\dim N(A^T) = m - r$

---

## The Four Subspaces for the Original Matrix $A$

1. **Row space**
   - Same as $R$ → same basis and dimension $r$
   - Row operations do not change the row space.

2. **Column space**
   - Dimension still $r$, but **different vectors** than in $R$.
   - **Column rank = row rank**
   - Pivot columns of $A$ form a basis.

3. **Nullspace**
   - Same as for $R$: same basis, same dimension $n - r$
   - Solutions to $Ax = 0$ are identical to those of $Rx = 0$

4. **Left nullspace**
   - Dimension $m - r$
   - Comes from solving $A^T y = 0$
   - Satisfies the same dimension rules as $R$

---

## Summary Table

| Subspace | Symbol | Defined by | Dimension |
|-----------|---------|-------------|------------|
| Row space | $C(A^T)$ | rows of $A$ | $r$ |
| Column space | $C(A)$ | columns of $A$ | $r$ |
| Nullspace | $N(A)$ | $Ax = 0$ | $n - r$ |
| Left nullspace | $N(A^T)$ | $A^T y = 0$ | $m - r$ |

## Examples

### Example 1: $A = [1\ 2\ 3]$
- $m = 1$, $n = 3$, $r = 1$
- Row space: line in $\mathbb{R}^3$ (dimension 1)
- Nullspace: plane $x_1 + 2x_2 + 3x_3 = 0$ (dimension 2)
- Column space: $\mathbb{R}^1$
- Left nullspace: $\{0\}$

Dimensions check:
- In $\mathbb{R}^n$: $1 + 2 = 3$
- In $\mathbb{R}^m$: $1 + 0 = 1$

---

### Example 2: 
$A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 2 & 4 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$

- $m = 2$, $n = 3$, $r = 1$
- Row space: same line through (1,2,3)
- Nullspace: plane $x_1 + 2x_2 + 3x_3 = 0$
- Column space: line through $(1,2)$
- Left nullspace: line through $(2, -1)$
- Dimensions:
  - $\mathbb{R}^n$: $1 + 2 = 3$
  - $\mathbb{R}^m$: $1 + 1 = 2$

---

### Example 3: Incidence Matrix of a Graph

Matrix $A$ (5 edges × 4 nodes):
- Each row corresponds to an edge, each column to a node.
- Each row has entries $-1$ and $1$ (start and end nodes).

Equations:  
$A x = b$ → voltage differences  
$A^T y = 0$ → current conservation (Kirchhoff’s Laws)

- $\dim N(A) = 1$: all node voltages equal → constant vector $(1,1,1,1)$
- $\dim C(A) = 3$: rank $r = 3$
- $\dim N(A^T) = 2$: two independent loops in the graph
- $\dim C(A^T) = 3$: three independent edge equations

---

## Big Picture
- **Row and column spaces** measure independence.
- **Nullspaces** measure freedom and orthogonality.
- **Dimensions depend only on rank ($r$)**:
  - $\dim C(A) = \dim C(A^T) = r$
  - $\dim N(A) = n - r$
  - $\dim N(A^T) = m - r$
- Together they perfectly partition $\mathbb{R}^n$ and $\mathbb{R}^m$.
